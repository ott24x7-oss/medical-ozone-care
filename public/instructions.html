Overview

The AOT-MD-520 converts pure medical oxygen into a precisely controlled stream of ozone (O₃) for ozone therapy. You set a mode (M1 or M2) and a level (L1–L6), meter the oxygen flow on the regulator, and the unit outputs a known ozone concentration between 4 and 92.5 mg/L. A built-in catalytic destructor safely neutralises any unused ozone.

Safety First

Never breathe ozone directly. Ozone is harmful to the lungs if inhaled. Always operate in a ventilated area and route unused ozone to the built-in destructor port.
Use medical-grade oxygen only. Do not use ambient air or industrial oxygen — ozone yield and purity depend on a clean O₂ feed.
  • Keep the unit dry, upright and on a stable surface during use.
  • Use only ozone-resistant tubing and accessories (supplied) — standard plastics degrade.
  • Do not block the destructor outlet or ventilation slots.
  • Ozone therapy must be administered by, or under the guidance of, a qualified practitioner.
  • Switch off and disconnect oxygen before connecting or removing accessories.

Setup & Connection

  1. Choose your oxygen source. Connect a medical oxygen cylinder (with the matching AOT regulator) or an oxygen concentrator with a correct flow meter.
  2. Fit the regulator. Attach the AOT oxygen regulator that matches your cylinder valve (870 pin-index, 540 threaded, or BN bull-nose).
  3. Connect oxygen to the generator. Run the silicone hose from the regulator outlet to the generator's O₂ inlet port.
  4. Connect the output. Attach tubing to an O₃ outlet, then to your application accessory (stone diffuser for liquids, or male luer lock / 3-valve lock for other methods).
  5. Connect the destructor. Route any unused-ozone line to the O₃ destructor port so excess ozone is neutralised, not released.
  6. Power up. Plug in the 100–240V adaptor to the power socket. The unit runs on 110–220V AC at just 15W.
Tip: keep all tubing as short and kink-free as practical for stable flow and the most accurate concentration.

Operation

  1. Select the mode. Press the M1 / M2 switch. M1 covers levels L1–L4 (lower range); M2 covers L5–L6 (higher range).
  2. Set the oxygen flow. Adjust the flow on your regulator (1/32, 1/16, 1/8, 1/4, 1/2, 3/4, 1 L/min). Lower flow = higher concentration.
  3. Choose the level. Press an L1–L4 button (M1) or use the L5/L6 knobs (M2) for the concentration you need.
  4. Wait for stable output. Allow about 30 seconds for the ozone concentration to stabilise before delivering therapy.
  5. Read the value. Cross-reference your flow + level on the concentration chart below to confirm the exact mg/L.
Example: For ~30 µg/ml, press M1, then L2, and set the regulator to 1/8 L/min (= 30.2 µg/ml).

After Use & Care

  • After therapy, let the unit run oxygen-only for a few seconds to flush residual ozone, then power off.
  • Disconnect the oxygen supply and depressurise the regulator.
  • Rinse and dry reusable accessories; replace stone diffusers and tubing periodically.
  • Wipe the housing with a dry or slightly damp cloth — never immerse the unit.
  • Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.

Troubleshooting

No / low ozone outputCheck oxygen flow is set on the regulator and the O₂ inlet hose is connected and unkinked.
Concentration seems offConfirm the correct mode (M1/M2) and level, and that flow matches the chart. Shorten tubing if very long.
Smell of ozone in the roomEnsure the destructor line is connected and the destructor port is not blocked; improve ventilation.
Unit will not power onVerify the 100–240V adaptor is firmly seated in the power socket and the outlet is live.
Still stuck? Contact Medical Ozone Care support before opening the unit — there are no user-serviceable parts inside.
